What is Treaty 4

  • Treaty 4, also known as the Qu'Appelle Treaty, is one of the numbered treaties negotiated between the Canadian government and Indigenous nations in the late 19th century.
  • Signed on September 15, 1874, at Fort Qu'Appelle in what is now southern Saskatchewan.
  • The treaty covers a vast area that includes parts of present-day southern Saskatchewan, southwestern Manitoba, and a small portion of southeastern Alberta.
